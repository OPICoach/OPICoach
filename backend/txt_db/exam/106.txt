This response provides a lesson on various English words and expressions, focusing on 'rain', 'butterfly', 'butter', and 'fly'.

Key vocabulary and expressions explained:
- Rain: light rain (drizzling), heavy rain (pouring).
- Butterfly: 나비 (Na-bi).
- 'I have butterflies in my stomach': means feeling nervous (synonym: 'I am very nervous').
- Difference between 'nervous' (feeling) and 'nerve-racking' (describing something that causes nervousness, e.g., 'It is nerve-racking').
- 'Butterfingers': describes someone who drops things easily.
- 'Butterface': (slang, potentially offensive) describes a woman with a good body but an unattractive face.
- 'Fly': refers to a zipper (especially on pants).
- 'He's so fly': (older slang) means he is cool or stylish.
- 'Butterfly effect': the concept that small initial changes can have large effects later.
